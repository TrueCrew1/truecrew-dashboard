# UpKeep

> File under `knowledge/sources/`. Schema: `knowledge/reference/knowledge-schema.md`.

## Snapshot

Mobile-first CMMS expanding into a broader “asset operations” suite: CMMS + EHS/safety, IoT/Edge, fleet, training, custom apps, and AI. Core still work orders, PM, assets, parts.

## Facts checked

- **Product hub:** positions CMMS as start, then safety (incidents, inspections, audit trails, corrective actions), IoT triggers, fleet, Learn, Studio. Source: https://upkeep.com/product/
- **PM:** schedules by time, meter, or sensor; auto-creates WOs with procedures/parts; mobile checklists, photos, signatures; offline for basements/remote sites. Source: https://upkeep.com/product/preventive-maintenance/
- **Offline help:** plan-gated cache limits (Professional vs Enterprise); update status/tasks/checklists offline; drafts for new WOs offline. Source: UpKeep help article on offline mode (linked in frontmatter).

## Strengths / gaps vs TrueCrew

- **Strong:** PM automation narrative; offline documented in help center; safety/EHS bolted onto same platform.
- **Gap:** suite sprawl (CMMS+EHS+IoT+fleet) vs TrueCrew’s narrow supervisor command-center bet. “Audit trails” appear in EHS marketing — competitive claim, not legal advice.

## Implications for TrueCrew

UpKeep shows incumbents sell **exception queues + PM compliance %** as the daily rhythm. TrueCrew Today should answer the same job without becoming a full CMMS.
