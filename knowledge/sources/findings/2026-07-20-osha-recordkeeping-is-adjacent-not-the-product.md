# OSHA recordkeeping is adjacent not the product

> File under `knowledge/sources/`. Schema: `knowledge/reference/knowledge-schema.md`. Not policy until validated/promoted.

## Context

Public OSHA recordkeeping overview only. **Not legal advice.** Does not cover MSHA or DOT.

## Finding

OSHA’s 29 CFR Part 1904 injury/illness recording and reporting regime is a **distinct employer obligation** (Forms 300/300A/301, severe injury reporting, some electronic submission). It is adjacent to—but not the same as—CMMS work-order audit trails.

## Evidence

- OSHA Recordkeeping landing: recording, reporting fatalities/severe injuries, electronic submission via ITA. https://www.osha.gov/recordkeeping
- Recording page: covered employers keep occupational injury/illness records on Forms 300/300A/301 or equivalent; retention and posting rules summarized on OSHA pages. https://www.osha.gov/recordkeeping/recording

## Implications for TrueCrew

Do not market Today/Ops as “OSHA compliance software” because of work history alone. Link-out or partner for true 1904 workflows; keep product language about **work evidence** honest.
