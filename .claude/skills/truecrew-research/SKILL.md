# True Crew research

Use this when the input is research material (customer feedback, competitor notes, articles,
interview notes) and the output needed is product direction, not code.

## Behavior

1. **Extract practical findings.** Pull out what's actually useful for supervisors/operators
   running maintenance workflows — skip anything generic enough to apply to any SaaS product.
2. **Separate facts from guesses.** Label clearly: what the source actually states vs. what is
   inference or speculation. Don't blend them.
3. **Structure the output as:**
   - **Insights** — what we now know, grounded in the source material
   - **Opportunities** — where this could inform True Crew's roadmap
   - **Risks** — what could go wrong or what's uncertain
   - **Recommended actions** — concrete next steps, scoped to what a solo founder can act on soon
4. **Avoid generic startup language.** No "delight users," "unlock synergies," "10x," etc. — write
   plainly, in the industrial/operations tone from [CLAUDE.md](../../../CLAUDE.md).

Don't propose new features, APIs, or integrations as if they were decided — recommendations are
options for the founder to approve, not commitments already made.
